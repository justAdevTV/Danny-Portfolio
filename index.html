My mentor, David Schlocker, kindly delegated the task to me. I began the project with a research phase. Here, I learned about the companies manufacturing process and its unique design aesthetic. My primary goal for this step was to identify what made MOQU an outlier.
MOQU Overview and Elevator [After a few weeks of creative feedback from David, I was able to complete a Brand Overview for our client. Our next step was to compose an E-Blast (or a personalized pitch to media writers).
Once the Brand Overview was completed, the next step was to create an E-Blast. This electronic pitch would be sent, via email, to our editorial contacts. I chose pictures for the E-Blast and worked with our graphics team to finalize the pitch. On July 1, 2017, DRS emailed the E-Blast to our editorial contacts.
The E-Blast was a success. My use of counter-narrative storytelling landed MOQU into several accredited design publications; including the Los Angeles Times…

Buster & Punch was one of my favorite clients to work with at the firm. Their products range from lighting to jewelry and all the way to motorcycles. DRS was tasked with maximizing B&P’s performance at a LA based trade show.
The Westedge Design Fair is an annual trade show located in the Santa Monica Airport’s Barker Hangar. For one weekend in October, the hanger floor becomes a hub for the contemporary design community. I was brought in to assist Buster & Punch with their outreach to the Los Angeles design community (You can actually see our booth at the bottom right corner of this photo).
My job was to be a salesman for the brand. I helped to educate booth go-ers on Buster & Punch’s product, philosophy and team. My job also included getting contact information from (and and pouring whiskey shots for) designers, architects and media professionals who showed interest in Buster & Punch.
My performance as a salesman was recognized by my mentor, David Shlocker; so, he gave me the another task, be interviewed by media group Convo by Design. I was quite nervous for this interview, but I am glad David gave me this public speaking opportunity
By the end of the trade show, I had obtained roughly 50 contacts for Buster & Punch, was interviewed under the spotlight, spoke with (and gave a shot to) Design Milk journalists (whom subsequently published an article on the brand), and, much more importantly, gained a valuable sales experience that I deeply cherish.

Young Americans for Liberty (YAL) is a national collegiate association advocating for Constitutional freedoms. The club provided me a lane to express my unique political beliefs while at the same time giving me a platform to develop my branding techniques. My work as the clubs media coordinator aided the club in becoming YAL’s largest chapter on the west coast.
As the clubs media coordinator, I was tasked with designing a flyer for the club. In early September of 2017, I designed this piece around a few key design techniques. I used the an iconic image (the Statue of Liberty) to equate our clubs values with the American Dream. The color yellow represents the ideas of the Libertarian Party, who also uses the color for their campaigns. Then I expressly wrote the the clubs beliefs and the time of our meetings in a clear and concise format. These different techniques came to create a progressive first impression on potential club members.
Alongside the publication of the flyer, I published a column in my school's newspaper advocating for the removal of our campus “Free Speech Zones”. This column, alongside the flyer allowed our club to dominate our campuses media and physical environment.
After the publication of both projects, I lead a group of club members to post the flyers around campus. With a comprehensive media presence at our school, we were able to recruit nearly 30 members in our first month as a club. Both projects were a big hit and contributed to a large showing at our first club meeting.

On October 19, 2017, Pierce College hosts the annual “Day of Politics”. The day is comprised of student debate as well as presentations by legislators. As a member of the political community at my school, I elected myself to debate during the event. (Left: California State Senator, Henry Stern)
The topic of debate was health care. In front of a crowded auditorium, I argued that government entanglement has caused healthcare prices to skyrocket. Although an unpopular opinion, I was elected as winner of the debate through my use of primary sources and audience participation.
The event was not only successful for the club, but for myself personally. Our club had the opportunity to meet prominent legislators in the Los Angeles community (in this photo: former Green Party Mayor of Santa Monica, Michael Feinstein). I was able to refine my public speaking skills and engage in meaningful political dialogue. Our club ended the Day of Politics having recruited more members and solidify our prominence in the Pierce College political community.
